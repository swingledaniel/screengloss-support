ScreenGloss

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 6, 2026

Overview

ScreenGloss processes screenshots only when you choose to send a screenshot to the app, usually through an iOS Shortcut. The app is designed to recognize text in that screenshot, show selectable text boxes, and optionally translate or explain the recognized text.

The current App Store version of ScreenGloss processes everything on your device: text recognition and translation run locally, and the app does not send your screenshots or recognized text to any server. The sections below about cloud features describe optional capabilities in development builds or possible future versions.

Information Processed

The app may process screenshot images, recognized text, target language settings, translation settings, and selected text. ScreenGloss does not sell personal information and does not use third-party advertising SDKs.

On-Device Processing

Text recognition and local translation options are processed on your device using system or on-device machine learning frameworks. These local features do not require ScreenGloss to send your screenshot text to a developer-operated server.

Cloud Features

If you choose a cloud translation option or request an AI explanation, the recognized text from your screenshot may be sent to a cloud provider so the provider can return a translation or explanation. ScreenGloss sends the text needed for the selected feature and does not intentionally send the full screenshot image for normal text translation or explanation features.

Cloud providers may process requests under their own terms and privacy policies. Current or planned providers include Google translation or Gemini services and Anthropic Claude services.

Storage

ScreenGloss may store app settings, selected translation preferences, and credentials or tokens in the iOS Keychain. The app does not intentionally maintain a long-term history of your screenshots or recognized text. A production backend may keep limited operational logs for security, abuse prevention, rate limiting, and debugging.

Contact

For privacy or support questions, please use the support link on the ScreenGloss support page.
